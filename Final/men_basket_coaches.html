Head Coach: Eric Olen


Following nine seasons as an assistant coach with the UC San Diego men's basketball team, Eric Olen was named the program's 12th head coach on Oct. 1, 2013.

Over the past nine seasons, Olen has played an integral part in all aspects of running the program and has served as the Tritons' recruiting coordinator. His primary duties have also included developing UCSD's post players.

Olen joined the UC San Diego coaching staff as a second assistant under head coach Bill Carr for the 2004-05 season. Just one year later, he was promoted to first assistant.

During his tenure at UCSD, Olen has helped guide the Tritons to some of the program's most successful campaigns.

In 2007-08, UCSD posted an 18-12 overall record, its best mark since joining the NCAA Division II ranks prior to the 2000-01 season. The Tritons won their first CCAA Tournament Championship and earned a berth in the NCAA Division II West Regional for the first time in program history.

In 2008-09, the Tritons finished with an overall record of 17-11, recording back-to-back winning seasons for the first time at the Division II level.

UCSD recorded its first winning season at the D-II level in 2005-06, ending the year with a 15-14 overall record. The Tritons finished fourth in the CCAA that season with a league tally of 12-8, marking the program's first winning record in conference play.

While at UCSD, Olen has helped coach players to 14 All-CCAA selections and two Tritons have garnered league Freshman of the Year accolades.

As a player at Spring Hill College in Mobile, Ala., Olen helped the Badgers to three straight appearances in the NAIA National Tournament, including trips to the NAIA Elite Eight in 2000 and 2001. He graduated in 2004 with a degree in finance.

Assistant Coach: Gerred Link


Gerred Link was named an assistant coach with the UC San Diego men's basketball program in September of 2012.

In his first season at UCSD, Link helped guide the Tritons to a berth in the California Collegiate Athletic Association (CCAA) Championship Tournament. Guards James McCann and Tyler McGrath were both named to the All-CCAA Second Team.

Link's coaching career began at Sonoma State as a student assistant in 2010. He rapidly progressed to a full-time assistant that same year and was responsible for providing tactical and technical skills instruction to the guards. In addition, Link was involved in recruiting, compliance, scouting, film management, strength and conditioning, fundraising and budgeting.

While in Rohnert Park, Link was also the co-director of Sonoma State's basketball camps.

Link's playing resumé includes both domestic and international competition.

He captained the 2008-09 Sonoma State squad and was named the team's Most Valuable Player that season. He was the tabbed the Seawolves' Most Inspirational Player following the 2007-08 campaign.

From 2004-06, Link competed at Grossmont College in El Cajon, where he was an All-Pacific Coast Conference selection in both basketball and tennis. He was a member of the baseball team at Los Angeles Pierce College in 2004.

Link traveled overseas to play for the Aix-Marseille Area Select Team in Aix-en-Provence, France, from August 2009 through January of 2010.

The Los Angeles native earned a Bachelor's Degree in kinesiology with an emphasis in exercise science from Sonoma State in 2011. He is currently pursuing a Master's in kinesiology with a concentration in biomechanics and exercise physiology.

Assistant Coach: Terry Ryan


Terry Ryan was named an assistant coach with the UC San Diego men's basketball program in October of 2013.

Ryan arrived at UCSD after serving as the head women’s basketball coach at San Diego's Canyon Crest Academy from 2004-12, and then as the assistant boy’s coach for the 2012-13 season. In 2009, he won the San Diego High School Sports Association CIF Champion Coach’s Award.

Prior to his work at Canyon Crest, Ryan was the boy's freshman head coach at nearby Torrey Pines High School, where he guided the team to league championships all four seasons (2000-04). In his final season, the team posted a school-record 20-2 mark.

Ryan was also the coach and co-president of the AAU Del Mar Select Basketball Club from 1999-2006.

Ryan’s college coaching career began at Marymount University as an assistant men’s coach in 1988. He then moved on to Ocean County College and was the men’s head coach from 1991-93, leading the team to a No. 9 national ranking and a 16-7 overall record, the best in the program's history.

While in Maryland, Ryan also spent time coaching at Montgomery College (1993-95). He started out as an assistant men’s coach for two seasons, helping the team to a 21-9 record and a third-place finish at the national tournament in 1994. In Ryan's final year at Montgomery, he served as the women’s head coach, with his team pacing the nation in scoring and finishing the season ranked No. 1 nationally. He was named the NJCAA District F and NJCAA Region XX Coach of the Year.

In addition, Ryan spent three years at the College of Lake County (1996-98) and one season at the University of San Diego (1998-99).

Ryan earned a Bachelor of Arts in social and behavioral science from Johns Hopkins University in 1986. He went on to get his MBA at Marymount University in 1990.
